## Slide 01 — Opening
**Label:** Digital Nepal Transformation · Opening

### Nepal is at a very important **moment.**

People are expecting more **transparency**, more **accountability**, and better systems.

But today, even though we are investing in digital infrastructure — the outcomes are still limited.

This is not because of **lack of effort** — it is because of **how we are building** those systems.

---

## Slide 02 — The Core Problem
**Label:** The Core Problem

### We are paying more, but *owning* **less.**

- The same types of software are **built again and again** across different ministries
- Systems **do not communicate** with each other — fragmentation grows with every new project
- Many systems become **inactive after a few years** — a graveyard of unused tools
- Most data is **locked in PDFs** — collected but never used for real decision-making

> "We are paying multiple times for the same solutions — but not getting full value from them."

## Slide 04 — A Shift in Thinking
**Label:** A Shift in Thinking

### From isolated *projects* to shared national **digital assets.**

**Today's Approach:**
- x Each ministry builds its own version of the same system
- x Vendors own the systems — government loses control
- x Pay again every time requirements change
- x Costs repeat with every new project, every year

**The New Model:**
- -> Build once, reuse across all ministries nationwide
- -> Government owns the code, deployment, and roadmap
- -> Modify open source code — no new contracts needed
- -> Cost of second deployment approaches zero

`Build once` | `Use everywhere` | `Improve continuously`

## Slide 06 — The Model
**Label:** Nepal Open Digital Commons · The Model

### A *simple*, structured flow that **changes everything.**

1. **Ministry publishes problem** — No procurement — a clear problem statement
2. **National Problem Registry** — Open, transparent, visible to all
3. **Contributors build solutions** — Students, devs, startups, diaspora
4. **Expert panel validates** — Architecture, security, scalability
5. **Government adopts & scales** — National digital asset — reused forever

`Transparent` | `Expert-governed` | `Reusable` | `Open source code` | `Government owns final systems`

---

## Slide 07 — The Pilot Proposal
**Label:** The Pilot Proposal

### Start *small.* Prove value. **Scale fast.**

**Phase 1 · Month 1–2: National Problem Registry**
3–5 pilot ministries log real problems into an open platform visible to all contributors across Nepal

**Phase 2 · Month 2–4: Open Data Dashboard**
Extract usable data from PDFs. Simple dashboards for decision-makers. Public progress tracking

**Phase 3 · Month 4–6: Full Demonstration**
One complete cycle: problem published -> solution contributed -> expert review -> government adoption

> "We are not proposing a big change all at once — we are asking to start with one system and build a model Nepal can scale."
